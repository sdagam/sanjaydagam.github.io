Activities

Leadership, competitions, and community involvement at Brookfield Academy.

School Leadership

🔭

Varsity Science Bowl — Captain

I was named team captain as a junior in 2025 and led the team to a 3rd place finish at the state science bowl competition. As a sophomore I earned a varsity spot and helped lead the team to 5th place at state.

🧮

Math Club — 2× President

I served as president sophomore and junior year, running meetings, organizing contest participation, and teaching math concepts. I boosted attendance by over 75% and led the club to 1st place in the Purple Comet Math Meet, 2nd in the MSOE contest, and 2nd in the Wisconsin Math League contest.

🗳️

Class President — 2× Elected

I was elected class president freshman and sophomore year. I represented my classmates at school events, sent biweekly updates, and organized class-wide events including a Secret Santa.

🇫🇷

French Club — Vice President

I work alongside the president to run meetings and club operations. I raised over $500 in club revenue through sales and organized a new member event that boosted membership by 50%.

Volunteer & Community Service

🍎

Kinship Community Food Center

I have volunteered weekly at Kinship Community Food Center since June 2024, distributing food, stocking shelves, and handling clean-up — nearly two years of consistent service.

🎾

Volunteer Tennis Coach — MTEF

I provide tennis instruction to 30 low-income students ages 7–13 through the Milwaukee Tennis and Education Foundation, and have been actively coaching since June 2024.

🌱

Wisconsin Students for Students

I co-founded Wisconsin Students for Students, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit providing free math enrichment to girls and low-income middle schoolers across southeastern Wisconsin. Together with our volunteers, we have logged over 2,500 hours, taught 300+ classes, and reached 200+ students.

💚

Mental Health Advocacy

I lead awareness campaigns and peer support training in schools to reduce stigma and improve access to mental health resources for teenagers.
