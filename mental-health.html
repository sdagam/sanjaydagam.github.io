Mental Health Initiative

Two initiatives — one school, one state — built from personal experience with the cost of stigma.

Why This Matters — A Personal Story

This work is personal. A family member of mine — someone I love — attended the same school I do. She was bullied, relentlessly, until the weight of it became more than she could carry. She attempted suicide.

When she came back to school, I hoped things would be different. They weren't. Instead of compassion, she faced something that shocked me: students taunting her about what she had been through. Mocking her for it. The very experience that had nearly taken her life became ammunition for cruelty. She came back to a school that didn't know how to hold her — and in some cases, actively made it worse.

I watched that happen, and I couldn't stop thinking about it. Not just what she went through, but why. Why is it that someone at their most vulnerable — someone who has just fought to stay alive — can come back to their school community and be met with shame instead of support? The answer is stigma. Stigma is what turns a mental health struggle into something to hide. Stigma is what gives bullies a weapon. Stigma is what keeps students from asking for help before they reach a breaking point.

I can't undo what happened. But I can build something that works against the conditions that allowed it to happen — in my own school, and across Wisconsin. That is why I am building two initiatives: a student mental health club at Brookfield Academy, and Speak Up Wisconsin, a statewide organization dedicated to eliminating mental health stigma among students across the state. This isn't a project. It's a commitment.

Current Projects

School Initiative

Minds Matter at BA — Brookfield Academy

A student mental health club at Brookfield Academy focused exclusively on reducing stigma and creating a safe space for honest conversation. Meetings center on peer-led discussion, mental health education, and school-wide awareness campaigns — with strict ground rules that keep the space supportive and student-driven. Independent of any outside organization per school policy.

Statewide Initiative

Speak Up Wisconsin

A student-founded statewide organization dedicated to eliminating mental health stigma across Wisconsin. School chapters across all five regions run local anti-stigma campaigns, peer speaker events, and distribute a free Conversation Starter Kit. An annual Stigma Index survey tracks progress statewide. Tagline: "Because silence isn't strength."
