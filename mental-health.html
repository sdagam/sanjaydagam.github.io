Mental Health Initiative

Building evidence-based programs to support youth mental health and reduce stigma.

Why This Matters

Mental health challenges among teenagers have reached unprecedented levels, yet stigma and lack of resources prevent many young people from seeking help. I believe that evidence-based interventions, combined with peer support and awareness programs, can make a meaningful difference.

My initiative focuses on creating safe spaces in schools, training peer advocates, and developing accessible resources that empower teenagers to support themselves and each other.

1 in 5 teens experience mental health challenges
60% don't receive treatment
11 min avg time to reach peer support
💚

Program Pillars

🏫

Safe Spaces Program

Training teachers and students to create supportive environments in schools where mental health conversations are normalized.

🤝

Peer Advocacy Network

Equipping student leaders with the skills to recognize signs of distress and connect peers with appropriate resources.

📱

Digital Resources

Developing accessible apps and content that provide evidence-based mental health education and self-help tools.
