Mental Health Initiative

Two initiatives rooted in a personal belief: that no student should face their hardest moments alone or in silence.

Why This Matters — A Personal Story

This work is personal. A family member of mine — someone I love — was bullied until the weight of it became more than she could carry. She attempted suicide.

When she returned to school, some peers responded not with compassion but with taunting — using her experience as something to mock rather than something to respect. I watched that happen, and it stayed with me. Not as anger at any one person or place, but as a deep question: why does our culture make it so hard to struggle openly? Why does asking for help still feel like something to be ashamed of?

The answer, I came to understand, is stigma. Stigma is what turns a mental health struggle into a secret. It is what keeps students silent when they most need to speak up. And it is what allows cruelty toward someone at their most vulnerable to feel, to the people doing it, somehow acceptable. It is not a school problem or a family problem — it is a cultural problem, and it starts young.

I can't undo what happened. But I can do something about the conditions that make it more likely to happen to someone else. That is what this initiative is about — two efforts, one at my school and one across Wisconsin, working toward the same goal: a world where no student has to face their hardest moments alone or in silence.

The Initiatives

School — Brookfield Academy

Minds Matter at BA

A student-led mental health club at Brookfield Academy built around one core idea: that students support each other best when they feel safe enough to be honest. Meetings are peer-led and follow strict ground rules — what is shared stays in the room, no one is required to disclose anything, and anyone in crisis is always connected to a professional. The club runs school-wide awareness campaigns and a monthly discussion series on topics students actually care about: stress, social pressure, identity, and how to help a friend who is struggling.

Statewide — Wisconsin

Speak Up Wisconsin

A student-founded statewide organization with one mission: eliminating mental health stigma among Wisconsin youth. School chapters across five regions run local anti-stigma events and peer speaker assemblies. A free Conversation Starter Kit is available to any school. Every year, Speak Up Wisconsin publishes a Stigma Index — an anonymous statewide survey measuring whether attitudes are actually changing. Because good intentions without data are just good intentions.

App — Available Now

Empower: Mental Health App

An iOS app built specifically for teenagers. Empower gives students a private space for mood tracking, guided breathing, coping strategies, and mental health education — tools designed to be used in the moment, not just in a crisis. Built with peer feedback and available free on the App Store.
